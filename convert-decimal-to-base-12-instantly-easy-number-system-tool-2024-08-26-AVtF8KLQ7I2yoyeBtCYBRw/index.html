How It Works

Converting a decimal number to base-12 might sound complicated, but it’s actually pretty straightforward. Here’s the formula broken down in plain English:

  1. Separate the number: Split your decimal number into its whole number and fractional parts (e.g., 123.45 becomes 123 and 0.45).
  2. Convert the whole number: Divide the whole number by 12 repeatedly and note the remainders. These remainders become the digits of your base-12 number, read from bottom to top.
  3. Convert the fractional part: Multiply the fractional part by 12 repeatedly. The whole number part of each result becomes the next digit in the fractional base-12 number.
  4. Combine the results: Put the whole number and fractional parts together to get your final base-12 number.

For example, the decimal number 15 converts to base-12 as follows: 15 divided by 12 is 1 with a remainder of 3, so the base-12 result is "13".
