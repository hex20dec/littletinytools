Locale-Specific Alphabetical Sorter

Why does sorting text in different languages feel like solving a puzzle? You’ve got a list of words, but when you sort them, the results don’t look quite right. That’s because every language has its own rules for alphabetical order. Enter the Locale-Specific Alphabetical Sorter—a tool that sorts your text perfectly, no matter the language. Whether you're organizing a multilingual glossary, prepping a presentation, or just satisfying your inner word nerd, this tool makes sorting a breeze. Say goodbye to manual headaches and hello to perfectly ordered lists!

Sort your text alphabetically based on the rules of a specific locale.

The Locale-Specific Alphabetical Sorter works like a multilingual librarian. It takes your list of words or phrases, splits them line by line, and sorts them according to the rules of the locale you choose. Here’s how it works:

  • Step 1: Enter your words or phrases, one per line.
  • Step 2: Select the locale (e.g., English, Spanish, French).
  • Step 3: Click "Sort" and watch your list transform into a perfectly ordered sequence.

For example, sorting "café," "chaise," and "château" in French will follow the French alphabet rules, placing "chaise" before "château." It’s that simple!
