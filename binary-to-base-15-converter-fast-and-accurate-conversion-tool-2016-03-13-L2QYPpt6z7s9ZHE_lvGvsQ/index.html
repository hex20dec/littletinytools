How It Works

Our Binary to Base-15 Converter works by first converting your binary number into its decimal (base-10) equivalent. Think of it as translating binary into a language everyone understands. Once we have the decimal number, we then convert it into base-15, which uses digits 0-9 and letters A-E to represent values 10-14. Here’s a simple breakdown:

  1. Start with your binary number (e.g., 1011).
  2. Convert it to decimal by adding up the values of each digit multiplied by 2 raised to its position (e.g., 1*2^3 + 0*2^2 + 1*2^1 + 1*2^0 = 11).
  3. Divide the decimal number by 15 and record the remainder. Repeat this step until the decimal number is 0.
  4. The remainders, read in reverse order, give you the base-15 result (e.g., 11 in decimal becomes B in base-15).
