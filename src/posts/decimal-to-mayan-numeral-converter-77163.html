How It Works

The Mayan numeral system is based on a vigesimal (base-20) structure, which means it uses 20 as its foundation instead of the decimal system's base-10. Here's how the tool works in simple terms:

  1. Step 1: The tool takes your input (a positive whole number like 42) and breaks it down into powers of 20. For example, 42 is 2 × 20 + 2 × 1.
  2. Step 2: Each part of the breakdown is represented by Mayan symbols. A dot (•) stands for 1, and a horizontal bar (—) represents 5. So, 42 becomes two bars (10) and two dots (2) stacked vertically.
