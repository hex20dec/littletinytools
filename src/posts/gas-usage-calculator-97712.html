How It Works

The Gas Usage Calculator uses a straightforward formula to estimate your gas costs. Here’s the breakdown:

  • Distance ÷ Fuel Efficiency = Gallons or Liters Used
  • Gallons or Liters Used × Gas Price = Total Cost

For example, if you’re driving 300 miles in a car that gets 25 MPG and gas costs $3.50 per gallon, the calculation would be:

  • 300 miles ÷ 25 MPG = 12 gallons
  • 12 gallons × $3.50 = $42.00

That’s it! No complicated math, just quick and accurate results.
