Temperature Expansion Tool

The Temperature Expansion Calculator is a free online tool that calculates the change in length of a material due to a change in temperature, making it an essential resource for engineers, materials scientists, and physicists. By inputting the initial temperature, final temperature, coefficient of expansion, and initial length, users can quickly determine the change in length and final length of the material, allowing for accurate predictions and calculations in various fields, including engineering, materials science, and physics.

Calculate the change in length of a material due to a change in temperature.

Result:

Formula: ΔL = α * L * ΔT

Example: If the initial temperature is 20°C, the final temperature is 50°C, the coefficient of expansion is 0.000012, and the initial length is 100m, the change in length will be 0.36m.

How it Works

The Temperature Expansion Calculator uses the formula ΔL = α * L * ΔT, where ΔL is the change in length, α is the coefficient of expansion, L is the initial length, and ΔT is the change in temperature. This formula allows users to calculate the change in length of a material when its temperature changes.
