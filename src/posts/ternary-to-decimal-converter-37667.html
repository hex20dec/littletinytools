How Does the Ternary to Decimal Converter Work?

Think of ternary numbers as a series of place values, just like decimal numbers, but instead of powers of 10, they use powers of 3. Each digit in a ternary number represents a value multiplied by 3 raised to the power of its position (starting from 0 on the right). For example, the ternary number 1201 can be broken down like this:

  • 1 × 3³ = 27
  • 2 × 3² = 18
  • 0 × 3¹ = 0
  • 1 × 3⁰ = 1

Add them all up: 27 + 18 + 0 + 1 = 46. So, 1201 in ternary equals 46 in decimal. Our tool does all this heavy lifting for you in a flash!
