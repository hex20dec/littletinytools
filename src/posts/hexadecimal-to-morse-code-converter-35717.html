How it Works: The Science Behind the Conversion

The binary to decimal conversion is based on the positional notation of binary numbers. Each binary digit (or bit) is multiplied by the corresponding power of 2, and the results are summed to produce the decimal equivalent. The formula for this conversion is:

Decimal = (bn × 2n) + (bn-1 × 2n-1) + ... + (b0 × 20)

Where bi is the i-th binary digit, and n is the number of bits.
