How It Works

Converting AEDT to EST might sound like rocket science, but it’s actually pretty straightforward. Here’s the breakdown:

Step 1: AEDT is 11 hours ahead of Coordinated Universal Time (UTC+11), while EST is 5 hours behind UTC (UTC-5). That means there’s a 16-hour difference between AEDT and EST.

Step 2: When you enter a time in AEDT, the tool subtracts 16 hours to give you the corresponding EST time. For example, if it’s 10:00 AM AEDT, subtracting 16 hours gives you 6:00 PM EST (from the previous day).

Step 3: The tool automatically handles daylight saving adjustments, so you don’t have to worry about seasonal changes messing up your calculations.
