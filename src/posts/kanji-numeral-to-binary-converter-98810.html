How Does It Work?

The Kanji to Binary Converter works by translating Kanji numerals into their decimal equivalents first. Each Kanji character (like 一 for 1 or 十 for 10) is mapped to a specific number. Once the Kanji numeral is converted to a decimal number, the tool then converts that decimal number into binary. Binary is just a way of representing numbers using only 0s and 1s, which computers understand. For example:

  • Kanji: 一 → Decimal: 1 → Binary: 1
  • Kanji: 十 → Decimal: 10 → Binary: 1010
  • Kanji: 百 → Decimal: 100 → Binary: 1100100
