How It Works:

The tool takes your binary number (a sequence of 0s and 1s) and follows a simple two-step process:

  1. Step 1: Convert the binary number to its decimal (base-10) equivalent. Think of this as translating the binary into a number you’re more familiar with.
  2. Step 2: Take that decimal number and convert it into base-18. Base-18 uses digits from 0 to 9 and letters A to H to represent values 10 to 17.

For example, if you input 1010 (binary), the tool first converts it to 10 (decimal), then to A in base-18. Easy, right?
