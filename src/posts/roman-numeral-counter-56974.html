How it Works

The Roman Numeral Counter uses a simple yet effective formula to convert integers to Roman numerals. This formula is based on the standard rules of Roman numeral conversion, where each numeral is replaced by a combination of seven letters: I, V, X, L, C, D, and M.

The formula works as follows: each integer is broken down into its decimal equivalents, and then replaced by the corresponding Roman numeral. For example, the number 4 is written as IV, because it is one less than 5 (V). Similarly, the number 9 is written as IX, because it is one less than 10 (X).
