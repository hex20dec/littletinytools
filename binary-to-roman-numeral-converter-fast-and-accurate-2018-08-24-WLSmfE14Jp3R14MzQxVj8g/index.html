How Does It Work?

The tool uses a simple two-step process to convert binary numbers into Roman numerals:

  1. Step 1: The binary number (e.g., 101.101) is converted into its decimal equivalent. This involves breaking it into its integer and fractional parts, calculating each separately, and then combining them.
  2. Step 2: The decimal number is then converted into a Roman numeral using a mapping system that assigns specific Roman symbols to decimal values. For fractions, special symbols like ½ or ¼ are used to represent fractional parts.
