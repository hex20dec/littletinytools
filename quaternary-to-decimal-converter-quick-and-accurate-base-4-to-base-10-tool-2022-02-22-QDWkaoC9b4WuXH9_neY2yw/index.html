How It Works

Converting a quaternary number to decimal might sound fancy, but it’s actually pretty straightforward. Here’s the secret sauce:

  1. Each digit in the quaternary number represents a power of 4, starting from the rightmost digit (which is 4⁰).
  2. Multiply each digit by 4 raised to the power of its position (starting from 0).
  3. Add up all the results, and there you have it—a decimal number!

For example, let’s convert 123 (base-4) to decimal:

  • 1 × 4² = 16
  • 2 × 4¹ = 8
  • 3 × 4⁰ = 3

Add them up: 16 + 8 + 3 = 27 (decimal). Easy, right?
