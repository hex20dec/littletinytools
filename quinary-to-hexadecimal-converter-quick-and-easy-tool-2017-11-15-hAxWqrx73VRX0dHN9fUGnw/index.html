How It Works

The Quinary to Hexadecimal Converter works in two simple steps:

  1. Step 1: Convert Quinary to Decimal - Each digit of the quinary number is multiplied by 5 raised to the power of its position (starting from 0 on the right). The results are added together to get the decimal equivalent.
  2. Step 2: Convert Decimal to Hexadecimal - The decimal number is then converted into hexadecimal by dividing it by 16 repeatedly and mapping the remainders to their corresponding hexadecimal digits (0-9, A-F).

Here’s a quick example: Let’s convert the quinary number 1234 to hexadecimal:

  • Quinary to Decimal: 1×5³ + 2×5² + 3×5¹ + 4×5⁰ = 125 + 50 + 15 + 4 = 194
  • Decimal to Hexadecimal: 194 in decimal is C2 in hexadecimal.

And voilà! You’ve got your hexadecimal equivalent.
