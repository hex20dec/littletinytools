How It Works

The Character Density Counter works by scanning your text and calculating how often each character appears. Here's the simple formula it uses:

Character Density (%) = (Number of Times a Character Appears ÷ Total Number of Characters) × 100

For example, if the letter "e" appears 10 times in a 100-character text, its density would be 10%. Easy, right? Below is a sample table showing how the tool breaks down character counts and densities for a given text:
