How It Works

The tool follows a simple two-step process to convert quaternary (base-4) numbers to binary (base-2):

  1. Step 1: The quaternary number is converted to its decimal (base-10) equivalent. Each digit is multiplied by 4 raised to the power of its position (starting from 0 on the right).
  2. Step 2: The decimal number is then converted to binary by repeatedly dividing it by 2 and recording the remainders.

For example, the quaternary number 123 is first converted to decimal as (1 × 4²) + (2 × 4¹) + (3 × 4⁰) = 27. Then, 27 is converted to binary as 11011.
