How Does It Work?

The Anagram Word Counter uses a simple mathematical principle to calculate the number of possible anagrams for a given word or phrase. Here’s the breakdown:

  1. It counts the total number of letters in your input.
  2. It checks for repeated letters and calculates their frequency.
  3. Using factorial math (where 5! = 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1), it determines the total possible arrangements of the letters.
  4. If there are repeated letters, it adjusts the total by dividing by the factorial of the repeated counts to avoid duplicate anagrams.

For example, the word "listen" has 6 unique letters, so the total anagrams are 6! = 720. But a word like "banana" has repeated letters, so the formula adjusts for the repetitions (3 'a's and 2 'n's), giving you 6! / (3! × 2!) = 60 unique anagrams.
