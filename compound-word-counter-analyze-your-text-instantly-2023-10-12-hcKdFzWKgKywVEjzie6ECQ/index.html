    How It Works

    The Compound Word Counter uses a simple yet clever formula to identify compound words in your text. Here’s how it works:

    1. It splits your text into individual words, ignoring spaces and punctuation.
    2. It checks each word to see if it contains multiple smaller words or is split by hyphens or underscores (e.g., "mother-in-law" or "ice_cream").
    3. If a word meets the criteria, it’s flagged as a compound word and added to the results.
