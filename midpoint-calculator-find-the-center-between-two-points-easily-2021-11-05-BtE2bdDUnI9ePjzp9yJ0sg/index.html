How It Works

The Midpoint Calculator uses a simple formula to find the exact center between two points on a plane. Imagine you have two points: (x1, y1) and (x2, y2). The midpoint is calculated by averaging the x-coordinates and the y-coordinates separately. Here's the formula in plain English:

Midpoint = ((x1 + x2) ÷ 2, (y1 + y2) ÷ 2)

For example, if your points are (3, 4) and (7, 8), the midpoint would be ((3 + 7) ÷ 2, (4 + 8) ÷ 2) = (5, 6). Easy, right?
