Here’s how it works: The tool breaks down the molecular formula into its individual elements (like H for hydrogen and O for oxygen) and multiplies each by its atomic weight. Then, it adds up all the weights to give you the total molecular weight. For example, H₂O is calculated as (2 x 1.008) + (1 x 15.999) = 18.015. Simple, right?
