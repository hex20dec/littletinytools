How Does It Work?

The Hexadecimal to Duodecimal Converter works in two simple steps. First, it converts the hexadecimal (base-16) number you enter into its decimal (base-10) equivalent. Then, it takes that decimal number and converts it into duodecimal (base-12) format. Here's a quick breakdown:

  1. Step 1: Convert the hexadecimal number to decimal by multiplying each digit by 16 raised to the power of its position.
  2. Step 2: Convert the decimal number to duodecimal by repeatedly dividing it by 12 and using remainders to build the result.

For example, the hexadecimal number 1A converts to decimal as 26, and then to duodecimal as 22. It’s like magic, but with math!
