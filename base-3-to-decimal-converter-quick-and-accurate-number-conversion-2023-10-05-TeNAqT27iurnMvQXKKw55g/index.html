How It Works

The conversion from base-3 to decimal is simpler than it sounds! Every digit in a base-3 number represents a power of 3, starting from the rightmost digit (which is 30). For example, the base-3 number 120 is calculated as:

(1 × 32) + (2 × 31) + (0 × 30) = 9 + 6 + 0 = 15 (decimal).
