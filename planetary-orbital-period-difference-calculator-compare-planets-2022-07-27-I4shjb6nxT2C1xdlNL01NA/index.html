How It Works

Our calculator uses a simple formula to determine the difference between two planetary orbital periods:

Difference = |Orbital Period of Planet 1 - Orbital Period of Planet 2|

In plain English, you subtract the smaller orbital period from the larger one (or vice versa) and take the absolute value to ensure the result is always positive. This gives you the time difference in Earth days. Easy, right?
