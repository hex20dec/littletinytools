Calculate the time difference between two eclipse cycles.

The Eclipse Cycle Difference Calculator works by comparing two dates you provide—the dates of two eclipses. It calculates the exact difference in years, months, and days between them. Here’s how it does it:

  • 1. Subtract the first eclipse date from the second eclipse date to get the total time difference in milliseconds.
  • 2. Convert milliseconds into days by dividing by the number of milliseconds in a day (1000 * 60 * 60 * 24).
  • 3. Break down the total days into years, months, and remaining days for a clear, easy-to-understand result.

For example, if the first eclipse was on January 1, 2020, and the second was on June 15, 2023, the tool will tell you the difference is 3 years, 5 months, and 14 days. Simple, right?
