How It Works

The tool calculates the percentage change between two Celsius temperatures using a simple formula. Here's how it breaks down:

  1. Subtract the initial temperature from the final temperature.
  2. Divide the result by the absolute value of the initial temperature.
  3. Multiply by 100 to get the percentage change.

For example, if your initial temperature is 20°C and your final temperature is 30°C, the calculation would look like this:

((30 - 20) / 20) * 100 = 50%

This means there’s a 50% increase in temperature. Easy, right?
