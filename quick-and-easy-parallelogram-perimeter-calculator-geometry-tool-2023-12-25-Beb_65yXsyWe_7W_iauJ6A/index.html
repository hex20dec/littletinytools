How Does It Work?

The formula for calculating the perimeter of a parallelogram is straightforward. All you need are the lengths of its two adjacent sides (let’s call them Side A and Side B). The perimeter is simply 2 × (Side A + Side B). For example, if Side A is 5 units and Side B is 10 units, the perimeter would be 2 × (5 + 10) = 30 units. Easy, right?
