How Our Time Zone Converter Works

To convert time zones, our tool takes into account the differences in hours and minutes between the two zones. The formula for conversion can be thought of as: Target Time = Source Time + (Target Offset - Source Offset), where the offset is the difference in hours from Coordinated Universal Time (UTC). For example, to convert from Eastern Standard Time (EST) to Greenwich Mean Time (GMT), you would add 5 hours during standard time and 4 hours during daylight saving time.
