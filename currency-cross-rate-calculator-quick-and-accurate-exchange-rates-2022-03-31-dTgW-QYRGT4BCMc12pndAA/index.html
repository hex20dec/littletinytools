How It Works

The Currency Cross Rate Calculator uses a straightforward formula to determine the exchange rate between two currencies (Currency A and Currency B) based on a reference currency. Here's how it works:

Formula: Cross Rate = (Exchange Rate of Currency A to Reference Currency) ÷ (Exchange Rate of Currency B to Reference Currency)

For example, if 1 USD = 0.85 EUR (Currency A to Reference) and 1 USD = 0.75 GBP (Currency B to Reference), the cross rate between EUR and GBP would be 0.85 ÷ 0.75 = 1.1333. This means 1 EUR is equivalent to 1.1333 GBP.
