How It Works

Our tool takes a hexadecimal (base-16) number, converts it to its decimal (base-10) equivalent, and then translates that into a Roman numeral. Here's the breakdown:

  1. Step 1: The hexadecimal number (e.g., 1A.3F) is split into its integer and fractional parts.
  2. Step 2: The integer part is converted to decimal using base-16 calculations.
  3. Step 3: The decimal number is translated into a Roman numeral using standard Roman numeral rules (e.g., I, V, X, L, C, D, M).
  4. Step 4: If there’s a fractional part, it’s converted to a fraction and appended to the Roman numeral result.

For example, 1A.3F in hexadecimal becomes XXVI (31/100) in Roman numerals. Magic? Nope, just math!
