How It Works

Converting a hexadecimal number to base-7 might sound complicated, but it’s actually a simple two-step process. Here’s the magic formula:

  1. Step 1: Convert the hexadecimal number to its decimal (base-10) equivalent. Hexadecimal numbers use digits 0-9 and letters A-F, where A=10, B=11, and so on up to F=15.
  2. Step 2: Take the decimal number and convert it to base-7 by repeatedly dividing it by 7 and recording the remainders. The base-7 number is the sequence of remainders read in reverse order.

For example, let’s convert the hexadecimal number 1A to base-7:

  • Step 1: 1A in hex = (1 × 16) + (10 × 1) = 26 in decimal.
  • Step 2: 26 in decimal = (3 × 7) + 5 = 35 in base-7.
