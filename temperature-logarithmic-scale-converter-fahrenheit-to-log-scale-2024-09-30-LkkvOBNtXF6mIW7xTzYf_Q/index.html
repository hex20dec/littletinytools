This tool works by converting Fahrenheit temperatures into their logarithmic scale equivalents. Here's the simple formula it uses:

For example, if you input 32°F (the freezing point of water), the tool adds 459.67 to get 491.67 and then calculates the base-10 logarithm of that value. The result is approximately 2.69, which is the logarithmic scale equivalent.
