How It Works

The Non-ASCII Character Counter works by scanning your input text and identifying any characters that fall outside the standard ASCII range (0-127). Here’s the simple formula it follows:

Step 1: Input your text into the provided box.

Step 2: The tool checks each character’s Unicode value.

Step 3: If the value is greater than 127, it’s flagged as a non-ASCII character.

Step 4: The tool displays the total count and lists the specific characters found.

It’s that straightforward! No complex algorithms or technical jargon—just a quick and easy way to spot those elusive characters.
