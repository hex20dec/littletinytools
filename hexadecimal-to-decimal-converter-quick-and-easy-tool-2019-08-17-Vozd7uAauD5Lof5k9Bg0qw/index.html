How It Works

Converting hexadecimal to decimal might sound complicated, but it's actually pretty straightforward. Each digit in a hexadecimal number represents a power of 16, starting from the right. For example, the hexadecimal number 1A breaks down like this:

  • 1 (the first digit) is multiplied by 161 (16 to the power of 1), which equals 16.
  • A (the second digit) represents 10 in decimal, multiplied by 160 (16 to the power of 0), which equals 10.
  • Add them together: 16 + 10 = 26 (the decimal equivalent).
