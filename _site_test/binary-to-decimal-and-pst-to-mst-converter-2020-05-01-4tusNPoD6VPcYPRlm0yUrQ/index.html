How It Works

The binary to decimal conversion is based on the principle that each binary digit (or bit) in a binary number represents a power of 2. Starting from the rightmost bit (which is the 2^0 position), each bit's value doubles as you move to the left (2^1, 2^2, 2^3, etc.). To convert binary to decimal, you multiply each binary digit by its corresponding power of 2 and then sum all those values.

For the PST to MST conversion, it is crucial to understand that MST is one hour ahead of PST. Therefore, when converting a time from PST to MST, you simply add one hour to the original time. This principle guides the conversion process, ensuring that the resulting time in MST accurately reflects the one-hour difference.
