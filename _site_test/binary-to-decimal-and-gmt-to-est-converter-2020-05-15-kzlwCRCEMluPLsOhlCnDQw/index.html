How it Works

The binary to decimal conversion is based on the principle that each binary digit (0 or 1) represents a power of 2. The conversion is calculated by multiplying each binary digit by its corresponding power of 2 and summing the results. For example, the binary number 1010 can be converted to decimal as follows: (1 * 2^3) + (0 * 2^2) + (1 * 2^1) + (0 * 2^0) = 8 + 0 + 2 + 0 = 10.

The GMT to EST conversion is based on the time difference between the two time zones. GMT is 5 hours ahead of EST during standard time and 4 hours ahead during daylight saving time. For example, if it is 12:00 PM GMT, the equivalent time in EST would be 7:00 AM (during standard time) or 8:00 AM (during daylight saving time).
