How it Works

The Binary to Decimal Converter uses a simple formula to convert binary numbers to decimal equivalents. The formula is based on the positional notation of binary digits, where each digit's value is multiplied by 2 raised to the power of its position, counting from right to left and starting at 0. For example, the binary number 1010 can be converted to decimal as follows: (1 * 2^3) + (0 * 2^2) + (1 * 2^1) + (0 * 2^0) = 8 + 0 + 2 + 0 = 10.

IST to UTC Conversion

The IST to UTC Converter works by taking into account the time difference between Indian Standard Time and Coordinated Universal Time. IST is 5 hours and 30 minutes ahead of UTC, so the converter subtracts this offset from the input time to get the equivalent time in UTC. For example, if the input time is 14:30:00 IST, the converter will subtract 5 hours and 30 minutes to get the UTC time, which would be 09:00:00 UTC.
