How it Works

The Binary to Decimal Converter uses a simple formula to convert binary numbers to decimal. The formula is based on the positional notation of the binary number, where each digit's value is multiplied by 2 raised to the power of its position, counting from right to left and starting at 0. For example, the binary number 1010 can be converted to decimal using the following formula:

Decimal = (1 * 2^3) + (0 * 2^2) + (1 * 2^1) + (0 * 2^0) = 8 + 0 + 2 + 0 = 10

The CST to GMT Converter uses the time difference between the two time zones to convert CST to GMT. CST is 6 hours behind GMT, so the converter adds 6 hours to the CST time to get the GMT time. For example, if the CST time is 12:00 PM, the GMT time would be 6:00 PM.
