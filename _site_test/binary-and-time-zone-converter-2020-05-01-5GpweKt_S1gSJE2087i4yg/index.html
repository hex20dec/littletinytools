Binary and Time Zone Converter

The Binary and Time Zone Converter is a multifaceted tool designed to simplify two distinct conversion processes. On one hand, it functions as a binary to decimal converter, allowing users to easily translate binary numbers into their decimal equivalents. This is particularly useful in programming and computer science contexts where binary code is frequently used. On the other hand, it serves as a time zone converter, specifically enabling the conversion of Eastern Standard Time (EST) to Central Standard Time (CST), which is invaluable for coordinating events, meetings, or communications across different geographical locations. By combining these two functions, the tool offers a versatile solution for both technical and logistical needs.

How It Works

The binary to decimal conversion is based on the principle that each digit in a binary number represents a power of 2. The rightmost digit represents 2^0, the next one to the left represents 2^1, then 2^2, and so on. To convert binary to decimal, you multiply each binary digit by its corresponding power of 2 and then sum these products.
