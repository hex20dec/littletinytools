How it Works

The binary to decimal conversion is based on the principle that each binary digit (or bit) represents a power of 2. The decimal equivalent is calculated by multiplying each bit by its corresponding power of 2 and summing the results.

The MST to GMT conversion takes into account the 7-hour time difference between the two time zones. The conversion involves adding 7 hours to the MST time, considering the AM/PM designation to ensure accurate results.
