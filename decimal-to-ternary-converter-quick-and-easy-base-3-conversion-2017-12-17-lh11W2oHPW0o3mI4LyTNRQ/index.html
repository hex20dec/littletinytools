How It Works

Think of converting a decimal number to ternary as breaking it down into smaller chunks of 3s. Here’s the simple formula:

  1. Divide the decimal number by 3.
  2. Record the remainder. This becomes part of the ternary number.
  3. Repeat the process with the quotient until it reaches 0.
  4. Read the remainders in reverse order to get the final ternary number.

For example, if you want to convert the decimal number 10 to ternary:

  • 10 ÷ 3 = 3 with a remainder of 1.
  • 3 ÷ 3 = 1 with a remainder of 0.
  • 1 ÷ 3 = 0 with a remainder of 1.

Reading the remainders backward gives you 101 in ternary. Easy, right?
