How It Works

Converting a decimal number to sexagesimal (base 60) format is easier than you think! Here's the magic behind it:

  1. Degrees: The whole number part of your decimal value becomes the degrees.
  2. Minutes: Multiply the decimal part by 60. The whole number result is your minutes.
  3. Seconds: Take the remaining decimal from the minutes calculation and multiply it by 60 again. This gives you the seconds.

For example, if you enter 45.525, the tool calculates:

  • Degrees: 45°
  • Minutes: 31' (from 0.525 × 60)
  • Seconds: 30'' (from 0.5 × 60)

And the final result? 45° 31' 30''—easy peasy!
