How Does It Work?

Think of duodecimal numbers as a different way of counting, where instead of stopping at 9, we go all the way to 11 using letters like A and B. To convert a duodecimal number to decimal, the tool follows a simple formula:

Step 1: Start from the leftmost digit. Multiply it by 12 raised to the power of its position (starting from 0).

Step 2: Add the result to the next digit multiplied by 12 to the power of its position, and so on.

Step 3: Sum all these values together, and you’ve got your decimal number!

For example, the duodecimal number "1A" becomes 22 in decimal because: (1 × 121) + (10 × 120) = 12 + 10 = 22.
