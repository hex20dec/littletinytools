How It Works

The Word Density Counter works like a friendly detective for your text. Here’s the formula it uses:

  1. Step 1: It scans your text and counts how many times each word appears.
  2. Step 2: It calculates the total number of words in your text.
  3. Step 3: It figures out the density of each word by dividing its frequency by the total word count and multiplying by 100. This gives you the percentage of how often each word shows up.

For example, if the word “happy” appears 5 times in a 100-word text, its density would be 5%.
